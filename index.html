Indian cuisine is one of the world's unique cuisine. Street food is loved by more than 50% of the population. One of the delicious street food is Samosa. Scroll down to check out how this is made.

Prep Time

30 mins

Cooking Time

15 mins

Serves

5 people

Ingredients Required

For Dough

  • 2 cup maida
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ cup oil
  • ½ cup water

For Stuffing

  • 2 tsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • A pinch hing
  • 1 chilli (finely chopped)
  • ½ cup peas
  • ½ tsp kashmiri red chilli powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 4 potato(boiled & mashed)
  • 5 cashew(chopped)
  • 2 tbsp coriander (finely chopped)

Other Ingredients

  • water(for sealing)
  • oil(for deep frying)

Method of Preparation

  1. Boil green peas and potatoes in salted water in a pressure cooker until soft. After boiling, transfer them to a colander to remove excess water. Peel the potatoes and lightly mash them or finely chop them.
  2. While potatoes are boiling, let’s make a dough for crispy outer layer. Take maida, carom seeds, 3 tablespoons ghee (or oil) and salt in a wide mouthed bowl.
  3. Mix all the ingredients properly with hand. You will notice the mixture will turn crumbly after mixing. Now, add water in multiple small quantities and knead firm dough (harder than paratha dough). Cover it with wet muslin cloth/any cloth and let it set for 15-20 minutes.
  4. When the dough is setting, let’s start to make stuffing for samosa. Heat 2 tablespoons cooking oil in kadai over medium flame. Add cumin seeds and green-chilli-ginger paste. Stir and cook for a minute.
  5. Add boiled green peas and stir-fry for a minute. Add red chilli powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, dry mango powder and fennel seeds powder.
  6. Stir fry for a minute.
  7. Add chopped/mashed potato and salt.
  8. Mix them properly and cook for 2-3 minutes. Add coriander leaves, mint leaves and mix well.
  9. Turn off the flame and transfer stuffing mixture to a bowl. Let it cool for a while at room temperature.
  10. After 15-20 minutes, remove the wet cloth and knead the dough again until smooth. Divide it into 6 small portions and make round shaped balls from it.
  11. Take one dough ball and press it between your palms to flatten it a little. Put it over rolling board and roll it out into round shaped puri having approx 5-6 inch diameter. Cut it into two semi-circles.
  12. Spread water with the wet finger or a brush on the cut side edge to moisten the surface.
  13. Take one semi-circle and give it a shape of cone by folding it from both sides (overlapping one side over another) and pressing them to seal the sides.
  14. Put 2-3 tablespoons filling in the cone. Do not over fill the stuffing otherwise, you will not be able to seal the edges properly in next step.
  15. Wet the edges with moist finger or a brush and press it tightly with your thumb and index finger to seal them. Make all remaining samosas in same way.
  16. For deep frying, heat oil in a heavy based kadai (pan) over medium flame. When oil is medium hot, add 2-3 samosa (depending on the size of a pan) in it and reduce flame to low. Do not over crowd the oil to cook them evenly.
  17. Deep fry them on low to medium flame until they turn golden brown. Transfer fried samosas to a plate and serve with green chutney and tomato ketchup.
